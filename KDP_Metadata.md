## Book Description (Amazon Marketing Copy)

**From the outside, his life looked perfect. On the inside, he was dying.**

Shaun Critzer grew up in a loving, middle-class home, the son of a successful business owner in a picturesque Virginia town. He was a state champion bodybuilder, a Mr. Teen USA, a man who built a fortress of muscle to hide the gaping wounds within. But behind the armor, a childhood shattered by trauma and a spiraling addiction to alcohol and painkillers were leading him toward a bottom he never imagined.

*Crooked Lines: Bent, Not Broken* is the unflinching, brutally honest account of a man who lost everything. His home. His marriage. Custody of his children. His sanity. In a raw and vulnerable narrative, Critzer takes you from the bodybuilding stage to the psych ward, from the courtroom to the driver’s seat of a car he’d turned into a weapon, from the depths of suicidal despair to the quiet miracle of a second chance.

This is not a story about hitting rock bottom and finding overnight redemption. It’s a story about the messy, grueling, day-by-day work of recovery. It’s about learning to be a father again after your children have seen you at your worst. It’s about the gut-wrenching journey of processing childhood sexual abuse you’ve spent a lifetime burying. It’s about the quiet moments of grace that appear in the wreckage—a conversation in a coffee shop, a hug from a son who is learning to trust you again, the steady love of a woman who sees the man you are becoming, not the man you were.

The Portuguese saying *“Deus escreve direito por linhas tortas”* means “God writes straight with crooked lines.” For Shaun, those crooked lines were not mistakes; they were the directions home. This memoir is a testament to the power of radical honesty, a story of hope for anyone who feels too broken to be fixed, and a powerful reminder that our deepest wounds can become our greatest strengths.

If you have ever felt lost in the darkness of addiction, haunted by trauma, or convinced you are beyond saving, this book is a beacon. It is proof that your story isn’t over, that redemption doesn’t require a straight road, and that even the most crooked lines can lead to a beautiful life. You are not alone. You are not too far gone. And it is never too late to start over.

## Author Bio

Shaun Critzer is a recovered alcoholic, trauma survivor, entrepreneur, and father living in Charlottesville, Virginia.

After losing everything to addiction—his marriage, his home, custody of his children, and nearly his life—Shaun got sober on January 1, 2013. In the thirteen years since, he’s rebuilt his life from the ground up: remarried to his wife Shannon, co-parenting three children (Landon, Cameron, and Brody), and building businesses that help others find purpose and income in the digital economy.

A former Virginia state champion bodybuilder and Mr. Teen USA, Shaun knows what it’s like to build armor to hide pain. Today, he’s committed to helping others take off their armor and show up as their authentic selves.

Shaun is a member of Alcoholics Anonymous, an active sponsor to men in recovery, and a passionate advocate for childhood trauma survivors and families raising children with special needs. His son Landon has Chromosome 4P deletion syndrome, a rare genetic condition that has taught Shaun more about patience, gratitude, and unconditional love than any other experience in his life.

*Crooked Lines: Bent, Not Broken* is his first book.
